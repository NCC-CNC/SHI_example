# Session Handoff: 2026-07-16

## Summary

Second session on SHI_example. Shipped the last two MVP milestones, M4 (pixel
edit and restoration) and M5 (guided concept walkthrough), plus two small layout
tweaks from owner feedback. With this, the whole MVP (M0 through M5) is done: the

## Completed

M4: Pixel edit and restoration (PR #29, closes #15)

- Pure engine helper `applyCellEdits(grid, edits)` overlays a
  `cell index -> land cover` map without mutation (unit-tested).
- `GridView` gained an optional paint surface: cells become clickable and
  keyboard-operable (Enter/Space) when `onCellClick` is set.
- `EditPanel`: a land cover brush palette, an edit-count reset, and status text.
  Painting a cell back to its scenario type clears that edit.
- App applies edits to the shown year and recomputes every map and score live;
  the unedited "before" scores are computed only when edits exist.
- `RestorationSummary`: overall index and each species scored before vs after
  the edits (same baseline), with a signed, direction-colored delta. Appears
  once at least one cell is edited.
- Edits are an overlay on the displayed year (no forward propagation; that stays
  #21).

Layout tweaks from feedback (PR #30, #31)

- Moved the "Species Habitat Index" headline block to the top, directly under
  the controls and above the edit panel, so it stays in view while scrubbing and
  editing.

M5: Guided concept walkthrough (PR #32, closes #16)

- `tour.ts`: the seven steps (area -> time -> species -> overlap -> index ->
  edit -> restoration) as data, each with text, a highlighted region, and the
  app state it prescribes.
- `TourPanel.tsx`: a fixed bottom bar with step text, progress dots, and
  Back / Next / Close (last step reads Done).
- App: tour state plus a single-species display focus; entering a step applies
  its state and scrolls its region into view, closing clears the demo
  focus/edits. The index and overlap always use all three species even when a
  step focuses one card.
- Step 6 turns one forest cell barren; step 7 restores the developed corridor
  (column 3) that split the forest back to forest, reconnecting the fragments
  (overall index rises, no species loses).

## Current state

What works:

- Full pipeline is live and interactive: scrub 1993-2025, change the baseline,
  toggle connectivity and the overlap, hover any cell, paint the land cover map
  to edit it, and read the before/after restoration diff.
- The guided tour drives the real app through all seven steps and clears back to
  the full view on Done.
- 59 tests pass; CI green; deploy automated.

Architecture (unchanged in shape):

- `src/engine/` pure SHI logic (no React), unit-tested. `src/data/` typed
  content (land cover palette, species crosswalks, the synthetic scenario as a
  base grid + cumulative dated events, now with `applyCellEdits` in the engine).
  `src/ui/` React components, including `EditPanel`, `RestorationSummary`,
  `TourPanel`, and `tour.ts`.

Design decisions this session:

- The restoration demo (tour step 7) reconnects the developed corridor rather
  than the first-sketched "grassland to forest," because in the authored grid
  grassland is not adjacent to the forest, and the corridor is the faithful,
  unambiguously positive way to bridge the fragments. Rationale is written into
  `docs/design/02_example_scenarios.md`; the grassland-to-forest trade-off is
  left as a free-editing suggestion.

Known limits / simplifications (unchanged, by design):

- Synthetic data only; no real ESA CCI. No range or elevation clipping. Quality
  folded into suitability. Simple-mean aggregation. Grid is 10x10.
- Colors and layout verified headlessly (server-render smoke test plus a jsdom
  interaction test for the tour). No in-session visual screenshot; a human look
  at the deployed app is still the outstanding visual check, now including the
  tour bar and region-highlight styling.
